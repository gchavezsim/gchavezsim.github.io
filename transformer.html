Transformer Equivalent Circuit Calculation

Welcome to the Transformer Equivalent Circuit Calculation project! In this project, I created a MATLAB program that automatically calculates the equivalent circuit components of a transformer from its ratings and open and short circuit test results. This project exemplifies my passion for electrical engineering and my commitment to developing efficient solutions for complex engineering problems.

As an electrical engineering enthusiast, I was determined to simplify the process of calculating transformer equivalent circuit components. The program I developed prompts the user for transformer ratings and test results with a user-friendly interface. It also allows the option to save the input data for future use, making subsequent calculations more convenient.

The MATLAB code leverages the input data to create a Simulink model with the appropriate circuit components and their respective values. Notably, the program generates two circuit models, one from the low voltage side and the other from the high voltage side. This distinction is crucial as it ensures accurate modeling of the transformer's internal impedance when simulating complete circuits that involve the transformer.

By automating the equivalent circuit calculation, this project significantly streamlined my transformer-related assignments and lab work. It eliminated the need to perform manual calculations repeatedly, enabling me to focus on other aspects of my studies and research.

Key Features:

  • User-Friendly Interface: The MATLAB program features a simple and intuitive user interface, making it easy to enter transformer ratings and test data.
  • Simulink Model Generation: The program automatically creates two circuit models from the low voltage and high voltage sides, considering internal impedance.
  • Time-Saving Solution: By automating the equivalent circuit calculation, the project significantly enhanced the efficiency of transformer-related calculations and simulations.
  • Reusable Input Data: The option to save and reuse input data further accelerates future transformer circuit calculations.
